Right now, I am completing my Bachelors degree in Computer Science at the **University of Southampton**. First year tends to give students a `lot` of free time, which I am currently using to explore the subject on my own. The moment I reach the end of one rabbit hole, it always seems like a dozen more are propped up right in front of me... one of the most fun, but daunting, sides of this subject!

## Future

Research seems to be where a lot of my work has been headed - I have spent a large portion of my first year going through research projects or working with lecturers at my university with any extra work they might need offloading. The process of research is exciting, and a subject like Computer Science seems like the most fast-paced and dynamic landscape to embark on! In particular, reinforcement learning and evolutionary algorithms has been something I have been keen on researching, as well as biologically inspired models such as Spiking Neural Networks. 

In terms of Software and Software development, I have always been fond of C++ and performant code. An industry which facilitates this would be one where I would most want to spend my time in the future. Areas such as Low latency computing, High speed/scalable databases, rendering/graphics programming, or high performance computing seem to be directions I would like to be focused in. 

**`Of course, I am always poking into other parts of my subject, so this list is not mutually exclusive whatsoever!`**
